What is a Registered Ecological Garden?

A Registered Ecological Garden is a private residential property where the garden is actively managed for ecological improvement and recorded in a central registry. The property owner commits to measurable ecological practices—such as native plantings, habitat creation, soil restoration, and long-term stewardship—and these actions are documented, monitored, and aggregated over time. The registry serves as a permanent system of record, making private ecological work countable and allowing councils, planners, and researchers to understand the cumulative environmental value of residential land.

Why this exists

The problem

Across cities, significant ecological work occurs in private gardens—native plantings, carbon sequestration, habitat restoration, soil regeneration. Yet this work remains invisible to local government and urban planning systems. Councils cannot measure it, planners cannot incorporate it, and researchers cannot study it at scale. The result: private land doing ecological work goes uncounted in environmental policy, biodiversity targets, and climate planning.

The opportunity

If we treat private gardens as ecological infrastructure—measured, recorded, and aggregated—we create a new category of urban environmental asset. With standardised measurement and centralised registration, thousands of individual residential properties can be understood collectively.
